# Florida Chronic Disease Analysis

A county-level analysis of chronic disease prevalence across all 67 Florida counties using CDC public health data.

## What This Project Does
Analyzes 6 chronic conditions across Florida counties to identify high-burden areas and co-occurrence patterns relevant to clinical resource allocation and population health informatics.

## Key Findings
- Gadsden County has the highest diabetes prevalence in Florida at 19.2% — nearly 50% above the statewide average of 12.8%
- Sumter County leads in high blood pressure at 48.0%
- Gadsden County also tops obesity at 44.6%, showing strong co-occurrence with diabetes
- Diabetes and hypertension correlate strongly across counties, suggesting bundled care pathways are needed in high-burden areas

https://www.cdc.gov/places

1. Open the notebook in Google Colab
2. Run all cells top to bottom
3. Data pulls automatically from CDC — no download needed

## Clinical Informatics Relevance
Population health tools built on data like this help healthcare systems flag high-risk patients in EHR platforms like Epic and allocate resources to the counties that need them most.
